Montreal

Montreal is the second largest city in Canada after Toronto and Paris the city with the most French-speaking inhabitants in the world. It is Canada's cultural heart and as one of North America's oldest cities, Montreal has a fascinating history and beautiful architecture. In addition, it is because of its good restaurants. The Ville Souterraine is special; an immense underground complex of shops and restaurants.

Montreal is located on a crescent-shaped island in the Saint Lawrence River called Île de Montréal. The city was founded by the French in 1642 as part of their colony of New France.

Berlin

Berlin is the capital of Germany. The city has 3,644,826 inhabitants (2018). This makes Berlin the largest city in Germany.

Berlin is best known for the Berlin Wall. This wall divided Germany into 2 areas, West and East Germany.

The Berlin Wall was the most famous symbol of the Cold War and the division of Germany. At least 138 people have been killed in action at the Berlin Wall.

Stockholm

Stockholm is the capital and the biggest city of Sweden. Stockholm is also known as the 'Venice of the North' as ​​Stockholm has much larger waters in the city.

Stockholm is first mentioned in writings in 1252. The name comes from the composition stock (block of wood) and holm (island) and stands for the present-day Gamla Stan.

Stockholm has many museums, such as the

  • ABBA The Museum
  • Statelijk historisch museum
  • Zweeds Podiumkunstenmuseum
  • Nobelmuseum
  • Vasamuseum

Rio de Janeiro

Rio de Janeiro is a city located in the South-East of Brazil. The city is located on a wide bay, the Bay of Guanabara, and was the capital of Brazil until 1960.

In 2014 Rio de Janeiro was one of the locations where football was played during the World Cup

Christ the Redeemer is a huge statue of Jesus Christ on the 710 meter high Corcovado mountain. The statue itself is 38 meters high, and symbolizes the worldwide spread of the Christian message. The statue was officially unveiled on October 12, 1931, after approximately 10 years of construction.

Tokyo

Tokyo is the capital of Japan. Tokyo is the city that never sleeps. It is not strange because Tokyo has more than 9 million inhabitants.

In 2021, Tokyo was the home of the Olympic Games.

The city is known as a futuristic and modern city. Everywhere you go you see modern buildings.

This city has the largest concentration of banks, companies, headquarters, universities, museums, theaters and shops in Japan.
